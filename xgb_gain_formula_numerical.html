Step 1 — left child score
G_L² / (H_L + λ) = (−4)² / (3 + 1) = 16 / 4 = 4.0
Step 2 — right child score
G_R² / (H_R + λ) = (+2)² / (3 + 1) = 4 / 4 = 1.0
Step 3 — parent score (unsplit baseline)
(G_L+G_R)² / (H_L+H_R + λ) = (−4+2)² / (3+3+1) = 4 / 7 ≈ 0.571
Step 4 — full Gain calculation
Gain = ½ · [ 4.0 + 1.0 − 0.571 ] − γ
= ½ · 4.429 − 0.5
= 2.214 − 0.5
Gain = 1.714 → Gain > 0 ✓ Split accepted

◆ What the numbers reveal: The left child (G_L = −4) contributes much more to the Gain than the right (G_R = +2). The left child has a stronger, more consistent gradient signal — examples there all need the prediction moved in the same direction. The right child has mixed signals (g = +1, +1, 0) — less to gain from splitting there.
⚠ Interview trap: The Gain formula squares G (sum of gradients). This means a node where gradients cancel out (e.g. G = +3 and −3, so G_total ≈ 0) scores near zero — even if there are many examples. XGBoost won't split a node where the prediction is already roughly correct on average, even if individual examples are wrong in opposite directions.
✓ One-line summary for interviews: Gain = (benefit of left leaf) + (benefit of right leaf) − (cost of parent staying unsplit) − (penalty γ for making the split). If the split doesn't improve things by at least γ, it doesn't happen.
